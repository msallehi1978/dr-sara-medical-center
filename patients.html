New Patients

Your First Visit

We make every first visit as smooth and welcoming as possible. Here's exactly what to expect.

1

Arrival & Welcome

Arrive 10 minutes early. Our reception team will greet you, verify your details, and offer refreshments while you complete a short health questionnaire.

2

Comprehensive Exam

Your dentist will conduct a full oral health assessment including digital X-rays, gum health check, and screening for any immediate concerns.

3

Treatment Discussion

Your findings are explained clearly — with visuals. All treatment options, timelines, and costs are discussed openly before anything is agreed.

4

Your Care Plan

You leave with a personalised care plan, a written treatment estimate, and your next appointment booked — no pressure, no surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often.

For most adults, we recommend a check-up and professional cleaning every 6 months. Patients with active gum disease, a history of decay, or certain medical conditions may benefit from 3–4 monthly visits. Your dentist will advise the ideal recall interval at your first appointment.

Absolutely. Our digital X-ray system uses up to 90% less radiation than traditional film X-rays. The exposure is comparable to a short walk in sunlight. We follow DHA guidelines on imaging frequency and always use protective lead aprons. If you are pregnant, please let us know — we will avoid X-rays unless clinically essential.

Yes. We offer nitrous oxide (happy gas / laughing gas) for patients with mild to moderate dental anxiety, and oral sedation for those requiring deeper relaxation during longer procedures. IV sedation is available on referral through our partner anaesthesiology network. Please discuss your anxiety with us — we have many techniques to help.

Professional in-chair whitening results typically last 12–24 months depending on your diet and lifestyle habits (coffee, tea, red wine, smoking accelerate fading). We provide customised take-home top-up trays after every whitening treatment so you can maintain your results easily at home. Annual top-up sessions are very quick and affordable.

A veneer covers only the front surface of the tooth and requires minimal tooth preparation — ideal for healthy teeth that need aesthetic improvement. A crown encases the entire tooth and is used when the tooth is structurally compromised (large filling, cracked, or after a root canal). Your dentist will recommend which option is appropriate after a clinical examination.

We recommend a child's first dental visit by their first birthday or within 6 months of their first tooth appearing — whichever comes first. Early visits are not about treatment; they're about building a positive, comfortable relationship with the dental environment and giving parents guidance on brushing, diet, and fluoride.
